Clinical Instruments & Handover Tools

Professional inventory of nursing equipment and assessment frameworks.

QUICK NAV:

1. Physical Assessment Instruments

Stethoscope (Dual-Head)

Clinical Use: Auscultation. Diaphragm for high-pitched sounds; Bell for low-pitched sounds (murmurs).

Aneroid Sphygmomanometer

Clinical Use: Manual BP. The gold standard for validating automatic cuff readings in unstable patients.

Neurological Penlight

Clinical Use: Assessing PERRLA. Includes a millimeter scale for accurate pupil size measurement.

Reflex Hammer

Clinical Use: Testing DTRs. Essential for neuro checks and assessing Magnesium Sulfate toxicity in OB.

Pulse Oximeter

Clinical Use: Non-invasive SpO2 monitoring. Requires adequate peripheral perfusion for accuracy.

Tuning Fork

Clinical Use: Weber/Rinne tests for hearing; vibratory sensation tests for diabetic neuropathy.

2. Diagnostic & Point-of-Care (POC) Tools

Glucometer

Clinical Use: Monitoring blood glucose (CBG). Critical for managing DM and titrating Insulin.

Otoscope & Ophthalmoscope

Clinical Use: Visualization of the tympanic membrane (ear) and internal eye structures (retina).

Bladder Scanner

Clinical Use: Non-invasive ultrasound to measure post-void residual (PVR) volume.

Thermometer

Clinical Use: Core temperature measurement. Tympanic or Temporal preferred for speed.

3. Delivery & Therapeutic Equipment

Infusion Pump (IV Pump)

Clinical Use: Precise delivery of IV fluids and high-alert meds (Heparin, Insulin, Potassium).

Nebulizer

Clinical Use: Converts liquid medication into mist for inhalation. Used in Asthma/COPD exacerbations.

Incentive Spirometer

Clinical Use: Encourages deep breathing to prevent post-op atelectasis and pneumonia.

SCD Machine

Clinical Use: Sequential Compression Device. Prevents DVT in immobile patients by promoting blood flow.

4. Emergency & Airway Tools

Ambu Bag (BVM)

Clinical Use: Manual ventilation during respiratory arrest. Must be at every bedside in critical care.

Defibrillator / AED

Clinical Use: Delivering shocks to stop lethal arrhythmias like V-Fib or Pulseless V-Tach.

Suction & Yankauer

Clinical Use: Clearing oral secretions to maintain airway patency and prevent aspiration.

SBAR Handover

S: Situation
B: Background
A: Assessment
R: Recommendation

PPE Precautions

  • Standard: Hand hygiene for all patients.
  • Contact: Gown & Gloves (e.g., C.diff, MRSA).
  • Droplet: Surgical Mask (e.g., Flu, Pertussis).
  • Airborne: N95 Respirator & Negative Pressure Room (e.g., TB, Measles).
