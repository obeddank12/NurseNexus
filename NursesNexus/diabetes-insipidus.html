Diabetes Insipidus (DI)

ADH Deficiency & Fluid Volume Deficit Management

QUICK NAV:

1. Pathophysiology

DI is a disorder of the posterior pituitary gland characterized by a deficiency of Antidiuretic Hormone (ADH).

  • Neurogenic: Damage to the hypothalamus or pituitary (trauma/surgery).
  • Nephrogenic: Kidneys do not respond to ADH.

Without ADH, the distal tubules do not reabsorb water, leading to massive polyuria (up to 20L per day).

2. Clinical Manifestations

  • Polyuria: Excessive urination.
  • Polydipsia: Excessive thirst (craving cold water).
  • Dehydration: Poor skin turgor, dry mucous membranes, hypotension.
  • Low Urine Specific Gravity: Usually below 1.005.

3. Nursing Management

Intervention Rationale
Daily Weights Best indicator of fluid status.
Desmopressin (DDAVP) Synthetic ADH replacement.
Monitor Sodium Watch for Hypernatremia (concentrated blood).

Emergency Alert

Hypovolemic Shock: Monitor for tachycardia and dropping BP. If the patient cannot drink enough to keep up with output, they will crash.

DI vs. SIADH

Feature DI SIADH
ADH Low High
Urine Dilute Concentrated
Sodium High Low

Tip: DI is "Dry Inside", SIADH is "Soaked Inside".
