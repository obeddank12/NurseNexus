Diabetes Mellitus (DM)

Comprehensive Glucose Metabolism & Insulin Management

QUICK NAV:

1. Pathophysiology: Type 1 vs. Type 2

Diabetes is a chronic multi-system disease related to abnormal insulin production, impaired insulin utilization, or both.

Type 1 DM

Etiology: Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic Beta cells.

  • Total absence of endogenous insulin.
  • Ketosis-prone (DKA risk).
  • Requires exogenous insulin for life.

Type 2 DM

Etiology: Insulin resistance and/or decreased production.

  • Associated with obesity and sedentary lifestyle.
  • Non-ketotic (HHS risk).
  • Managed with diet, exercise, and oral agents.

The Classic Triad (The 3 P's): Polyuria (excess urine), Polydipsia (excess thirst), and Polyphagia (excess hunger).

2. Pharmacology: Insulin Management

Nursing Priority: Monitor for **hypoglycemia** during the Peak time of each insulin.

Type Onset Peak Duration Nursing Responsibility
Rapid (Lispro) 15 min 1-2 hr 3-5 hr Ensure food is present before injection.
Short (Regular) 30 min 2-5 hr 5-8 hr The only insulin for IV administration.
Intermediate (NPH) 1.5 hr 4-12 hr 12-18 hr Cloudy appearance. Roll, do not shake.
Long (Glargine) 1-2 hr None 24 hr Never mix in the same syringe.

3. Acute Complications & Crisis Management

Hypoglycemia (BG < 70)

Signs: Shakiness, diaphoresis, confusion.

Rule of 15: 15g simple carbs, wait 15 min, recheck BG. Repeat if needed.

DKA (Type 1 Crisis)

Signs: Fruity breath, Kussmaul respirations, BG > 250, Ketones in urine.

Tx: IV Fluids first, then Regular Insulin drip, monitor Potassium.

HBA1c Reference

Measures average glucose over 3 months.

  • Normal: < 5.7%
  • Target for DM: < 7.0%

Foot Care Teaching

  • Inspect feet daily with a mirror.
  • No bare feet (even at home).
  • Do not apply lotion between toes.
  • Cut nails straight across.

Mixing Insulin

"Clear before Cloudy"

Inject air into NPH, then Regular. Pull up Regular (Clear), then NPH (Cloudy).
