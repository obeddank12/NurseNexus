Hypertension (HTN)

The "Silent Killer": Classification, Pathophysiology, and Management

QUICK NAV:

1. ACC/AHA Blood Pressure Classifications

Hypertension is defined as a persistent systolic blood pressure (SBP) of 130 mmHg or more, or a diastolic blood pressure (DBP) of 80 mmHg or more.

Category Systolic (mmHg) Diastolic (mmHg)
Normal< 120AND < 80
Elevated120 - 129AND < 80
Stage 1 HTN130 - 139OR 80 - 89
Stage 2 HTN≥ 140OR ≥ 90

2. Target Organ Damage (TOD)

Chronic high pressure damages the endothelial lining of small and large vessels, leading to complications in four primary areas:

[Image showing complications of hypertension: Stroke, Retinopathy, Heart Attack, and Kidney Failure]

Cardiac & Vascular

  • LVH: Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (heart works too hard).
  • HF: Heart Failure.
  • PVD: Peripheral Vascular Disease.

Neuro, Renal & Retinal

  • CVA: Stroke/TIA risk increases significantly.
  • Nephropathy: Chronic Kidney Disease (check Creatinine).
  • Retinopathy: Retinal damage and vision loss.

3. Pharmacology: The "Big Three"

Class Example Nursing Priority
ACE Inhibitors Lisinopril Watch for Dry Cough and Angioedema (Emergency).
Beta-Blockers Metoprolol Check Heart Rate; hold if < 60 bpm. Masking of hypoglycemia in DM.
Calcium Channel Blockers Amlodipine Watch for peripheral edema and gingival hyperplasia.
Diuretics Hydrochlorothiazide Monitor Potassium levels and orthostatic hypotension.

4. Hypertensive Crisis

Urgency vs. Emergency

Crisis: SBP > 180 or DBP > 120.

Emergency: Evidence of target organ damage (e.g., chest pain, confusion). Requires IV Nitroglycerin or Nitroprusside. Goal: Reduce MAP by no more than 25% in the first hour.

Lifestyle: DASH Diet

Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension

  • High in fruits and vegetables.
  • Low-fat dairy.
  • Reduced saturated fat.
  • Sodium < 2,300mg (Ideal < 1,500mg).

Diagnostic Strategy

White Coat Syndrome: Fear of clinical settings causing spikes.

Nursing Note: Diagnosis requires two elevated readings on two separate occasions.

MAP Calculation

[SBP + (2 × DBP)] / 3

Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) > 65 is needed to perfuse vital organs.
