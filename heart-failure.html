Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)

Comprehensive Clinical Pathophysiology & Nursing Process

QUICK NAV:

1. Clinical Pathophysiology

Heart failure is not a disease itself, but a clinical syndrome resulting from structural or functional cardiac disorders that impair the ability of the ventricles to fill or eject blood.

Left-Sided Failure (Pulmonary)

Mechanism: Reduced Cardiac Output leads to blood backup in the pulmonary circulation.

  • Orthopnea: Difficulty breathing when lying flat.
  • PND: Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnea.
  • Pulmonary Edema: Moist crackles and pink, frothy sputum (Emergency).
  • S3 Gallop: Ventricular gallop heard on auscultation.

Right-Sided Failure (Systemic)

Mechanism: Failure of the right ventricle to pump into the lungs, backing up into the body.

  • JVD: Jugular Venous Distension at 45 degrees.
  • Dependent Edema: Swelling in ankles, feet, or sacrum.
  • Hepatomegaly: Enlarged liver and RUQ pain.
  • Anasarca: Severe generalized body edema.

2. Medical & Surgical Management

Pharmacology (The Core Four)

  • ACE Inhibitors: Dilate venules/arterioles to improve renal blood flow.
  • Beta-Blockers: Slow heart rate to reduce myocardial oxygen demand.
  • Diuretics: Furosemide (Lasix) to remove excess fluid volume.
  • Digoxin: Increases contractility (Check apical pulse for 1 full min).

Diagnostics

  • Echocardiogram: Measures Ejection Fraction (Normal: 55-65%).
  • BNP: Measures stretch of ventricles. (>400 = High).
  • CXR: Shows Cardiomegaly (enlarged heart).

3. Nursing Priorities (The Nursing Process)

Emergency Alert: Pulmonary Edema
If the patient develops sudden gasping for breath, cyanosis, and frothy sputum: 1. Sit them upright. 2. Administer High-Flow O2. 3. Call the MD for IV Diuretics.
  • Fluid Restriction: Usually 1.5L - 2L per day.
  • Sodium Restriction: 2g Sodium Diet (Avoid processed meats/canned goods).
  • Daily Weights: Same time, same clothes. Report 2-3lb gain in 1 day or 5lb in 1 week.
  • Skin Integrity: Edematous skin breaks easily; reposition every 2 hours.

Clinical Mnemonics
