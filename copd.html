Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

Airflow Limitation: Chronic Bronchitis & Emphysema

QUICK NAV:

1. Pathophysiology & Classification

COPD is a preventable and treatable disease characterized by persistent airflow limitation that is usually progressive and associated with an enhanced chronic inflammatory response.

Emphysema (Pink Puffer)

Primary Issue: Alveolar wall destruction and loss of elastic recoil.

  • Hyperinflation of lungs (Air Trapping).
  • Barrel Chest (2:2 ratio).
  • Thin/Frail appearance due to work of breathing.
  • Minimal cough, severe dyspnea.

Chronic Bronchitis (Blue Bloater)

Primary Issue: Excessive mucus secretion and airway inflammation.

  • Productive cough for >3 months in 2 years.
  • Cyanosis (due to hypoxemia).
  • Peripheral edema (Right-sided HF/Cor Pulmonale).
  • Frequent respiratory infections.

2. Advanced Assessment & Diagnostics

Clinical Manifestations

  • Tripod Position: Optimizes use of accessory muscles.
  • Pursed-Lip Breathing: Creates PEEP to keep alveoli open.
  • Polycythemia: High RBC count as body compensates for low O2.
  • Diminished Breath Sounds: Often heard in bases due to air trapping.

Diagnostic Gold Standards

  • Spirometry: Forced Expiratory Volume (FEV1/FVC < 70%).
  • ABGs: Chronic Respiratory Acidosis (High CO2, High HCO3 compensation).
  • Alpha-1 Antitrypsin: Checked in younger patients (Genetic link).

3. Pharmacology (The Respiratory Ladder)

Class Example Nursing Consideration
SABA (Rescue) Albuterol Monitor for tremors and tachycardia.
Anticholinergics Ipratropium Helps reduce secretions; dry mouth is common.
Corticosteroids Fluticasone Rinse mouth after use to prevent thrush (Candidiasis).
Methylxanthines Theophylline Narrow therapeutic range (10-20 mcg/mL); monitor for toxicity.

4. Nursing Priorities & Safety

The Hypoxic Drive: Patients with chronic hypercapnia rely on low O2 levels to trigger breathing. Over-oxygenating (SpO2 100%) can lead to apnea.
  • Oxygen Titration: Target SpO2: 88% - 92% via Venturi Mask (most precise).
  • Pulmonary Hygiene: Chest Physiotherapy (CPT) and "Huff" coughing to clear mucus.
  • Energy Conservation: Encourage rest periods before meals and after ADLs.
  • Nutrition: High-calorie, high-protein. Avoid gas-forming foods (bloating limits diaphragm movement).

Cor Pulmonale

When COPD leads to Right-Sided Heart Failure due to high pulmonary pressure.

  • Watch for: JVD, Hepatomegaly, and Peripheral Edema in COPD patients.

Pursed-Lip Teaching

  1. Inhale through nose (2 seconds).
  2. Exhale through "puckered" lips (4 seconds).
  3. "Smell the roses, blow out the candles."
